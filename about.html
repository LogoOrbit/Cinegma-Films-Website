The Company

Cinegma
Films LLC

Cinema as the medium. Enigma as the mystery within every frame.

Cinegma Films is an independent film production company founded in January 2024 and based in Karachi, Pakistan. Built around a single conviction — that Pakistani stories deserve to be told with precision, ambition, and without compromise — Cinegma Films operates as a creative home for narrative cinema that challenges, moves, and endures.

The name itself is a declaration of intent: Cinema as the medium, Enigma as the irreducible mystery at the heart of every frame. That philosophy shapes every production — from story selection to final color grade.

In its first year of operation, Cinegma Films produced work that screened across 9 countries, earned international recognition at festivals from Canada to India, Scotland to Kosovo, and achieved a theatrical release at Arena Cinemas, Karachi — an exceptional milestone for an independent student-led production company in Pakistan.

Cinegma Films operates across narrative short films, documentary, and is currently developing its first feature-length projects. The company also serves as a production arm for Silverlight Studios under Burrak Organization.

Where It All Started

Back When We Were
Just Students

Okay, real talk — before any of the awards, the theatrical release, or the festival runs, we were just a bunch of film students with borrowed gear, too many ideas, and zero budget. This is the stuff we made back in uni, starting from around our first semester all the way through.

Some of it is rough. Some of it we're honestly still proud of. We shot ad spec films because we couldn't afford real clients, recreated scenes from movies we loved, made a one-minute short just to prove we could, and even tried our hand at a talk show. None of it was perfect — but every single one of these taught us something we still use today.

This is where it all started. Scroll through and see how far we've come.

Syed Asad Raza Abidi · Karachi, Pakistan

Syed Asad
Raza Abidi

Filmmaker · Director · Editor · Colorist · Founder, Cinegma Films

Born on December 9, 2001 in Karachi, Pakistan, Syed Asad Raza Abidi is an independent filmmaker, director, editor, colorist, and the founder of Cinegma Films LLC. He holds a BS in Film & TV Production from Indus University, Karachi (2025), where his passion for storytelling was refined into a precision craft across years of hands-on production.

He founded Cinegma Films in early 2024 — an independent production banner built on an uncompromising creative vision. The name is a deliberate fusion of Cinema and Enigma, the two words that define his approach to layered, psychologically rich storytelling. His debut short film, Janjaal (Tussle), became one of the most decorated Pakistani short films in recent memory: shot in just 34 hours across Karachi, then directed, edited, color graded, VFX supervised, and mixed entirely in-house for a 5.1 surround theatrical premiere at Arena Cinemas, Karachi in January 2025. It went on to win 14 international awards and 22 festival selections across 9 countries.

Beyond Janjaal, Asad has directed, edited, and color-graded Portrait of Life (2025, thesis film — selected at Anymusic Film Festival, Spain), Maa (Mother) (2022 — Best Short Film at Cinephilia Flair IFF, selections in Iran, Egypt, and the USA), Main Aur Achu (2024, Silverlight Studios), and the documentary Urdu Bazaar Karachi. He has also served as DP and Editor on Phir Se (7T7 Productions) and Associate Director on Payam e Dil. In production: Zawaal and A Soldier Beside Me.

In addition to Cinegma Films, he serves as Film Director and Editor at Burrak Organization's Silverlight Studios, leading production of narrative Islamic short content.

My vision was much bigger than what we could afford. But that limitation forced us to be more precise, more intentional with every frame. We had 34 hours to make something people would sit still for in a cinema — and they did.

— Syed Asad Raza Abidi, Director, Janjaal

Karachi-based independent filmmaker Syed Asad Raza Abidi has announced that his debut short film Janjaal (Tussle) has received 14 international awards and 22 official selections at film festivals spanning nine countries. The film, a 23-minute Urdu-language psychological thriller produced under Cinegma Films, screened theatrically at Arena Cinemas, Karachi, in January 2025 — an uncommon milestone for a student-produced Pakistani short film.

The film's most recent honor came at the Toronto Fantasy/Sci-Fi Film Festival 2026, where Janjaal won Best Short Film — placing it among the few Pakistani short films to receive top recognition at a North American genre festival.

My vision was much bigger than what we could afford. But that limitation forced us to be more precise, more intentional with every frame. We had 34 hours to make something people would sit still for in a cinema — and they did.

— Syed Asad Raza Abidi, Director, Janjaal

The Making of Janjaal

It was still dark when the crew arrived at Sunhera Beach. They had police on the route — a necessary precaution for the early-morning coastal drive on the outskirts of Karachi — but somewhere along the way, the bikes disappeared into the darkness. The crew kept moving. They had no alternative.

What Abidi didn't know yet, standing on that dark beach with his student crew, was that the water wouldn't cooperate. He had researched the location obsessively — multiple visits, conversations with local fishermen, careful study of the tidal patterns. The water was supposed to reach the boats by 7 AM. By 9:30, the boats were still on dry sand.

We were all worried. But I had done my preparation. I knew the location. So when the situation changed, we could adapt instead of collapse.

— Syed Asad Raza Abidi

The water eventually came. They shot the scene. Janjaal — a psychological thriller starring veteran Pakistani actor Shamoon Abbasi — went on to win 14 international awards and 22 official festival selections. Screenplay by Ameer Hamza (co-written with Basil Hussain Zaidi), produced by Faizan Ahmed, cinematography by Ghayas Uddin Siddiqui. Every member of the core crew was a student.

Academic Origins

Abidi was in his sixth semester at Indus University Karachi's Film & TV program when he developed Janjaal. The premise was simple on paper — a detective investigating a mystery — but his ambition for the execution was significantly larger than what a student production budget could support.

Getting Shamoon Abbasi — a well-known face in Pakistani cinema and television — was a matter of an existing connection and a script that spoke for itself. Abidi had connected with Abbasi years earlier through his work in AI-generated content.

He read it and said yes. He's a straightforward person. He responds to the work.

— Syed Asad Raza Abidi

Post-Production & Theatrical Release

Post-production was the most demanding phase — completed under a fixed university deadline, as cinema screens had been booked for the theatrical premiere. Asad directed, edited, color graded, handled VFX, and mixed the film in 5.1 surround sound — a technical scope rarely managed by a single person on a student production.

The film screened theatrically at Arena Cinemas, Karachi, on January 5, 2025, before traveling to film festivals across Kosovo, Scotland, India, Iran, Spain, the United States, Canada, and Turkey.

Earlier Awards Include

Best Director and Best Short Film at the Cedar Film Festival (Pakistan); Best Director and Best Short Film at the Gandhara Independent Film Festival (Pakistan); Best Director and Best Short Film at the Kolkata International Short Film Festival (India); Best Short Film at the Pakistan International Film Festival; Best Director at the Indus Film Festival; Best Trailer at TMFF Scotland. Finalist at the Urbanite Art & Film Festival, New York.

Cinegma Films & Future Projects

In January 2024, Abidi founded Cinegma Films, an independent production company. The name is a deliberate fusion of 'cinema' and 'enigma' — the two words that best describe his filmmaking instinct. Currently in production under the Cinegma banner: Zawaal (a dark psychological thriller set in Karachi) and A Soldier Beside Me (a short film about two soldiers questioning their fate).

There are very talented filmmakers here making stunning work. And then they disappear. Not because they give up. Because there's nothing to hold them — no indie ecosystem, no support, no audience that's been trained to receive this kind of cinema. We need a new wave of Pakistani cinema. And we have to work together to build it. No one is going to do it for us.

— Syed Asad Raza Abidi
