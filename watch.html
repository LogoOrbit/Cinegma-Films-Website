The Films

Stream Pakistani Independent Short Films & Documentaries

Janjaal (Tussle, 2024–2025) is a 23-minute Pakistani psychological thriller directed by Syed Asad Raza Abidi. A police officer discovers a cursed golden pocket watch — each wish exacts a deadly price, unraveling fate, guilt, and obsession. Shot across Karachi in just 34 hours, the film went on to win Best Short Film at the Toronto Fantasy/Sci-Fi Film Festival 2026, Best Director at Cedar Film Festival 2026, Best Director and Best Short Film at KISFF India 2025, Best Short Film at PIFF 2025, Best Trailer at TMFF Scotland 2025, and Best Director at Indus IFF 2025 — 14 international awards in total.

Portrait of Life (2025) is a short drama and Syed Asad's graduation thesis film. A painter confronts what he has lost — and what remains. A meditation on love, memory, artistic obsession, and the irreversible passage of time. The film reflects the quiet devastation of watching connection erode.

Urdu Bazaar Karachi (2024) is a short documentary film exploring the historic book market in the heart of Karachi. Shot on location in the narrow lanes and centuries-old bookshops of Urdu Bazaar, the film examines books, literary culture, and the threats facing a city's collective memory. Directed, shot, and edited entirely by Syed Asad Raza Abidi, including aerial drone footage.

Main Aur Achu (2024) is a family drama short film produced by Silverlight Studios under Burrak Organization. The story follows Abdul, a young boy from a small village, and his beloved goat Achu — exploring themes of sacrifice, family bonds, and the meaning of Eid ul Azha. Directed and edited by Syed Asad Raza Abidi.

All films are produced in Pakistan, primarily in Karachi, in Urdu. Cinegma Films is committed to bringing Pakistani independent short cinema to international audiences — one story at a time.
